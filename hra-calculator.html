HRA Exemption Breakdown

What is HRA Exemption?

HRA = House Rent Allowance. Under Section 10(13A), salaried people can claim HRA exemption. Exemption is lowest of these 3:

HRA Formula:
1. Actual HRA Received
2. Rent Paid - 10% of Basic
3. 50% of Basic for Metro, 40% for Non-Metro

5 Key Rules for HRA Exemption

1

Salaried Only: Self-employed cannot claim HRA. Only salary people.

2

Rent Receipt: If rent > ₹1 Lakh/year, landlord PAN required.

3

Own House: If you live in own house, no HRA exemption.

4

Metro vs Non-Metro: Metro = 50% of Basic, Non-Metro = 40% of Basic.

5

Not in Old Regime: HRA exemption only in Old Tax Regime, not in New Regime.

Key Insight: To get max exemption, keep rent = 50% of salary for metro cities. Submit rent receipts to HR.

Disclaimer: HRA exemption available only in Old Tax Regime. For New Regime, HRA is fully taxable. Consult CA for exact filing.
