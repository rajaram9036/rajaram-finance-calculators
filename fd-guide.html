What is a Fixed Deposit (FD)?

A Fixed Deposit (FD) is a financial product offered by banks and eligible financial institutions where you deposit a fixed amount for a chosen period. In return, you earn interest at the applicable rate until maturity. The interest rate, tenure and terms vary by institution.

Potential Benefits of a Fixed Deposit

  • ✅ Offers a fixed interest rate for the chosen tenure, subject to the institution's terms.
  • ✅ Helps with disciplined savings over a fixed period.
  • ✅ Available with different investment tenures.
  • ✅ Suitable for people looking for predictable maturity values.
  • ✅ Can support short-term and long-term financial planning.

How Does an FD Work?

You deposit a lump sum amount for a selected tenure. Interest is calculated according to the institution's applicable rate and compounding rules. At maturity, you receive the principal along with the accrued interest, subject to applicable terms and taxes.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. What is the minimum amount required to open an FD?

The minimum deposit amount varies by the bank or financial institution. Please check the applicable terms before opening an FD.

2. Can I withdraw my FD before maturity?

Many institutions allow premature withdrawal, but it may be subject to terms, conditions or penalty charges depending on the institution.

3. Are FD interest rates fixed?

The interest rate is generally fixed for the selected FD at the time it is opened, subject to the institution's policies.

4. Is FD interest taxable?

Interest earned on Fixed Deposits may be taxable depending on the applicable tax laws and your individual circumstances. Please consult a qualified tax professional if needed.
