What is SIP?

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a simple way to invest a fixed amount of money regularly in mutual funds. Instead of investing a large amount at one time, SIP allows you to invest monthly and build wealth gradually over the long term.

Why Choose SIP?

  • ✅ Start investing with a small amount.
  • ✅ Build long-term wealth through disciplined investing.
  • ✅ Benefit from the power of compounding.
  • ✅ Reduce the impact of market volatility through regular investing.
  • ✅ Suitable for monthly financial planning.

How Does SIP Work?

You choose a monthly investment amount, select the investment period and an expected annual return. Your money is invested every month, and over time the returns may compound, helping your investment grow.

Example

If you invest ₹5,000 every month for 20 years at an assumed annual return of 12%, the total value may become significantly higher than the total amount invested because of compounding. This is only an illustration and not a guaranteed return.

Benefits of SIP

  • 📈 Disciplined investing habit
  • 💰 Potential long-term wealth creation
  • 📅 Flexible monthly investment
  • 🎯 Helps achieve financial goals
  • ⚖️ Reduces timing risk through regular investing

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. What is the minimum amount to start a SIP?

Many mutual fund schemes allow SIPs to start from ₹100 or ₹500. The minimum amount depends on the specific scheme.

2. Can I stop my SIP anytime?

Yes. In most cases, you can pause or stop a SIP according to the rules of your investment platform and the scheme.

3. Are SIP returns guaranteed?

No. SIP investments are linked to market performance, so returns are not guaranteed and can go up or down.

4. Is SIP suitable for long-term investing?

Many people use SIPs for long-term financial goals because regular investing and compounding can help build wealth over time.
