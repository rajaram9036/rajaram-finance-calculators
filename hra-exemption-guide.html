What is HRA Exemption?

House Rent Allowance (HRA) is an allowance that employers may provide to employees to help cover house rent expenses. Subject to the applicable income tax rules and conditions, a part of this allowance may qualify for tax exemption.

Important: HRA exemption is available only if the applicable legal conditions are satisfied. The amount of exemption depends on factors such as salary, HRA received, rent paid and city of residence.

Who Can Claim HRA Exemption?

  • ✅ Salaried employees receiving HRA as part of their salary.
  • ✅ Employees living in rented accommodation.
  • ✅ Employees who pay rent and can provide supporting documents if required.
  • ✅ Individuals who satisfy the applicable tax provisions.

Benefits of HRA Exemption

  • 🏠 May reduce taxable income if eligible.
  • 💰 Can help lower overall tax liability.
  • 📑 Encourages proper rent documentation.
  • 📊 Useful for tax planning.

How is HRA Exemption Calculated?

The amount of HRA exemption is determined according to the applicable income tax rules. Generally, the eligible exemption is based on the lowest of the prescribed values under the law. The exact calculation depends on your salary, HRA received, rent paid and whether you live in a metro or non-metro city.

📐 HRA Exemption Formula

The exempt HRA is generally calculated as the lowest of the following:

  • ✅ Actual HRA received from the employer
  • ✅ Rent paid minus 10% of salary (as defined under the applicable tax rules)
  • ✅ 50% of salary for metro cities or 40% for non-metro cities (subject to the applicable provisions)

Metro vs Non-Metro Cities

The percentage of salary considered in one part of the HRA calculation differs between metro and non-metro cities under the applicable tax rules. Make sure you select the correct city category while estimating your exemption.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Who can claim HRA exemption?

Eligible salaried employees who receive HRA as part of their salary and satisfy the applicable tax conditions may be able to claim HRA exemption.

2. Can I claim HRA if I live in my own house?

Generally, HRA exemption is intended for eligible employees living in rented accommodation. Eligibility depends on the applicable tax rules.

3. Are rent receipts required?

Employers or tax authorities may require rent receipts or other supporting documents depending on the applicable requirements.

4. Is the HRA Calculator 100% accurate?

The calculator provides an estimate based on the values entered. Your actual exemption depends on the applicable tax rules and your individual circumstances.

5. Can self-employed individuals claim HRA?

HRA is generally linked to salaried employment. Different tax provisions may apply in other situations.
