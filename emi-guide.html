What is EMI?

An Equated Monthly Installment (EMI) is the fixed amount paid every month to repay a loan. Every EMI includes both the principal amount borrowed and the interest charged by the lender. EMIs make loan repayment simple by dividing the total amount into affordable monthly installments.

Potential Benefits of EMI

  • ✅ Makes loan repayment easier through monthly installments.
  • ✅ Helps borrowers plan monthly expenses efficiently.
  • ✅ Fixed repayment schedule improves financial discipline.
  • ✅ Suitable for home, personal, vehicle and education loans.
  • ✅ Makes large purchases more affordable over time.

How Does EMI Work?

Your EMI depends on the loan amount, annual interest rate and repayment period. Each monthly payment gradually reduces your outstanding loan balance until the loan is fully repaid.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. What is EMI?

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is the fixed monthly amount paid by a borrower to repay a loan over a specified tenure.

2. How is EMI calculated?

EMI is generally calculated using the loan amount, annual interest rate and repayment tenure. An EMI calculator provides an estimate based on these inputs.

3. Can I reduce my EMI?

In some cases, selecting a longer loan tenure or making eligible prepayments may reduce the monthly EMI. The exact terms depend on your lender.

4. Is EMI always fixed?

Not always. Loans with fixed interest rates generally have fixed EMIs, while floating-rate loans may have changing EMIs or repayment periods.

5. Can I prepay my loan?

Many lenders allow partial or full prepayment. However, prepayment rules, eligibility and charges vary from lender to lender.
