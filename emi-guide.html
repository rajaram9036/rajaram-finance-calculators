What is EMI?

An Equated Monthly Installment (EMI) is a fixed amount paid every month to repay a loan. Each EMI consists of two parts: the principal amount borrowed and the interest charged by the lender. EMIs help borrowers repay loans in smaller monthly installments instead of making one large payment.

Potential Benefits of EMI

  • ✅ Makes loan repayment easier through monthly installments.
  • ✅ Helps borrowers manage monthly budgets efficiently.
  • ✅ Fixed repayment schedule improves financial discipline.
  • ✅ Available for home, personal, vehicle and education loans.
  • ✅ Makes large purchases more affordable over time.

How Does EMI Work?

Your monthly EMI depends on the loan amount, interest rate and repayment period. Every EMI gradually reduces the outstanding loan balance until the loan is fully repaid.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. What is EMI?

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is the fixed monthly amount paid by a borrower to repay a loan over a specified period.

2. Can I reduce my EMI?

Yes. In some cases, selecting a longer loan tenure or making partial prepayments may reduce your monthly EMI. The exact terms depend on your lender.

3. Is EMI always fixed?

Not always. Loans with fixed interest rates usually have fixed EMIs, while floating-rate loans may have changing EMIs or repayment periods.

4. Can I prepay my loan?

Many lenders allow partial or full prepayment. However, prepayment rules and charges vary from lender to lender.
