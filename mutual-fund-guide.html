What is a Mutual Fund?

A Mutual Fund pools money from multiple investors and invests it in a diversified portfolio of assets such as equity, debt or hybrid securities. Professional fund managers manage these investments to achieve the fund's objectives.

Potential Benefits of Mutual Funds

  • ✅ Professional fund management.
  • ✅ Diversification reduces investment risk.
  • ✅ SIP and Lumpsum investment options.
  • ✅ Suitable for long-term wealth creation.
  • ✅ Easy online investment and redemption.

How Do Mutual Funds Work?

Investors purchase units of a mutual fund. The fund manager invests the pooled money in different securities. The Net Asset Value (NAV) changes daily based on market performance.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. What is the minimum amount to invest?

Many mutual funds allow SIP investments starting from ₹500 per month, while minimum amounts can vary by scheme and fund house.

2. Is Mutual Fund investment safe?

Mutual Funds are market-linked investments. They carry investment risk, but diversification and long-term investing may help manage volatility.

3. What is SIP?

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) allows investors to invest a fixed amount regularly, helping build wealth through disciplined investing.

4. What is Lumpsum investment?

Lumpsum investing means investing a single amount at one time instead of making regular monthly investments.

5. Can I redeem Mutual Fund units anytime?

Most open-ended mutual funds allow redemption on any business day, although some schemes may have exit loads or lock-in periods.
