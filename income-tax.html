Old Regime vs New Regime FY 2025-26

From FY 2025-26, New Regime is default but you can choose Old Regime if deductions are more. Standard deduction ₹75,000 in New Regime and ₹50,000 in Old Regime.

Income SlabOld RegimeNew Regime
Up to ₹2.5 LakhNilNil
₹2.5L - ₹5L5%5%
₹5L - ₹7.5L20%10%
₹7.5L - ₹10L20%15%
₹10L - ₹12.5L30%20%
₹12.5L - ₹15L30%25%
Above ₹15L30%30%
Example: Salary ₹12 Lakh, 80C ₹1.5L, 80D ₹25K. Old Regime Tax = ₹65,000. New Regime Tax = ₹54,600. You save ₹10,400 in New Regime.

Key Deductions in Old Regime

1

80C: Up to ₹1.5 Lakh - PPF, ELSS, LIC, Home Loan Principal

2

80D: Up to ₹25K for self, ₹50K for senior parents

3

80E: Education Loan Interest - No Limit

4

24B: Home Loan Interest - Up to ₹2 Lakh

5

Standard Deduction: ₹50,000 for salaried in Old Regime

Key Insight: If total deductions > ₹3.75 Lakh, Old Regime is better. Otherwise choose New Regime.

Disclaimer: This calculator is for salaried individuals only. 4% Cess included. Tax slabs as per Budget 2025. Consult CA for exact filing.
