🏦 Recurring Deposit (RD) Guide 2026

A Recurring Deposit (RD) is a savings scheme offered by banks and post offices that allows you to deposit a fixed amount every month for a selected tenure. At maturity, you receive your total deposits along with the applicable interest.

1. What is a Recurring Deposit (RD)?

A Recurring Deposit helps individuals build savings through regular monthly deposits. It is suitable for people who want disciplined saving habits and a fixed maturity amount.

2. Benefits of RD

  • ✔ Encourages regular monthly savings.
  • ✔ Fixed interest rate for the selected tenure (subject to institution rules).
  • ✔ Low minimum monthly deposit.
  • ✔ Suitable for short and medium-term financial goals.
  • ✔ Available at many banks and post offices.

3. Eligibility

  • Indian Residents (as per institution rules).
  • Individuals, minors (through guardian) or joint account holders, subject to the bank's policy.
  • Valid identity and address proof.
  • KYC documents as required by the bank or post office.

4. Required Documents

  • 📄 Aadhaar Card or other valid identity proof
  • 🆔 PAN Card (if required)
  • 🏠 Address Proof
  • 📷 Passport Size Photographs (if required)
  • 🏦 Completed KYC documents as per bank or post office rules

5. How is RD Interest Calculated?

The maturity amount of an RD depends on the following factors:

  • 💰 Monthly Deposit Amount
  • 📈 Interest Rate
  • 📅 Deposit Tenure
  • 🏦 Compounding Method followed by the bank or post office

Higher monthly deposits and a longer investment period generally result in a higher maturity amount. The exact maturity value depends on the interest rate and compounding method used by the institution.

6. RD Maturity

At the end of the selected tenure, you receive your total deposited amount along with the interest earned. Some institutions may also offer renewal options after maturity.

7. Taxation

Interest earned on an RD may be taxable according to the applicable income tax laws. Tax rules can change, so check the latest regulations or consult a qualified tax professional if needed.

8. Tips Before Opening an RD

  • ✔ Compare RD interest rates offered by different banks.
  • ✔ Choose a monthly deposit amount that fits your budget.
  • ✔ Select a suitable tenure based on your financial goals.
  • ✔ Deposit every installment on time.
  • ✔ Read the bank's terms and conditions before opening the RD.

9. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1. Can I withdraw my RD before maturity?

Many banks allow premature closure, but penalties or reduced interest may apply depending on the bank's policy.

Q2. What is the minimum monthly deposit?

The minimum deposit amount varies from one bank or post office to another.

Q3. Is RD a safe investment?

Recurring Deposits offered by regulated banks are generally considered a relatively low-risk savings option, but you should always review the terms of the institution you choose.

10. Conclusion

A Recurring Deposit (RD) is a simple and disciplined way to build savings through regular monthly deposits. Before opening an RD account, compare interest rates, deposit tenure, premature withdrawal rules and other terms offered by different banks or post offices.
