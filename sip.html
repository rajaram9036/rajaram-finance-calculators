What is SIP? Systematic Investment Plan Explained

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a disciplined approach to investing in mutual funds. Instead of a lump sum, you invest a fixed amount monthly. This strategy helps investors benefit from rupee cost averaging and the power of compounding over the long term.

SIP Example: If you invest ₹5,000 per month for 10 years at 12% annual return, your total investment will be ₹6,00,000. But due to compounding, your maturity value can grow to approximately ₹11,61,695. That means a wealth gain of ₹5,61,695!

Why Choose SIP for Wealth Creation?

1

Start Small: Begin investing with as little as ₹500 per month and build wealth gradually.

2

Market Volatility Advantage: Rupee cost averaging reduces the impact of market fluctuations.

3

Power of Compounding: The earlier you start, the more your money grows due to reinvested returns.

4

Goal Based Investing: Ideal for long-term financial goals like child education, marriage, or buying a house.

Key Insight: Delaying your investment by just 5 years can reduce your final corpus by nearly 50%. Time in the market is more important than timing the market.
