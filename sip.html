What is SIP?

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a method of investing a fixed amount regularly in mutual funds. It helps build long-term wealth through disciplined investing and the power of compounding.

Power of Compounding

Returns earned over time may also generate additional returns, helping investments grow over the long term.

Rupee Cost Averaging

Regular investing across different market conditions can reduce the impact of market volatility.

Disciplined Investing

Investing every month encourages financial discipline and goal-based investing.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is SIP?

SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) is a way to invest a fixed amount regularly in mutual funds.

Is SIP better than FD?

SIP and FD serve different purposes. SIP is market-linked, while FD generally offers fixed returns. The suitable option depends on your goals and risk tolerance.

What is the minimum SIP amount?

Many mutual funds allow SIPs starting from ₹100 or ₹500, depending on the scheme.

Can I stop SIP anytime?

Yes. Most mutual fund platforms allow you to pause or stop future SIP installments.
