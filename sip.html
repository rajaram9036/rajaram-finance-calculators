What is SIP? Systematic Investment Plan Explained

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a disciplined way of investing in mutual funds. Instead of investing a lump sum, you invest a fixed amount every month. This helps you benefit from rupee cost averaging and the power of compounding.

SIP Example: If you invest ₹5,000 per month for 10 years at 12% annual return, your total investment will be ₹6,00,000. But due to compounding, your maturity value can grow to approximately ₹11,61,695. That means a wealth gain of ₹5,61,695!

Why SIP? 1. Start with small amounts like ₹500. 2. No need to time the market. 3. Builds financial discipline. 4. Ideal for long-term goals like retirement, child education, or buying a house.

The earlier you start, the bigger your corpus. Delaying by just 5 years can reduce your final amount by 50%.
